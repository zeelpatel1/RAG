1. What is Covariance?

Covariance measures how two variables change together.

If one variable increases and the other also tends to increase, the covariance is positive.
If one variable increases while the other tends to decrease, the covariance is negative.
If there is no consistent relationship, the covariance is close to zero.

How Covariance Works

Suppose:

Hours Studied	Marks
2	40
4	50
6	60
8	70

As study hours increase, marks also increase.

Covariance > 0

Now suppose:

Hours Playing Games	Marks
2	80
4	70
6	60
8	50

As gaming hours increase, marks decrease.

Covariance < 0

Interpretation
Covariance	Meaning
Positive	Variables move together
Negative	Variables move in opposite directions
Zero	No linear relationship
Limitation of Covariance

The magnitude is difficult to interpret.

Example:

Covariance = 15

Is 15 strong or weak?

You cannot tell because covariance depends on the units.

Changing units changes covariance.

Example:

Height in meters
Height in centimeters

The covariance changes dramatically even though the relationship is identical.

This is the biggest drawback.
